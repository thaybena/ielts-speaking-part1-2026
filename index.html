HOW TO USE The A.R.E Framework & 3-Second Rule
THE A.R.E FRAMEWORK

Every answer in IELTS Speaking Part 1 should follow this three-part structure. Aim for 2–4 sentences total per answer.

A
ANSWERGive your direct answer immediately. Do not hesitate or repeat the question.
R
REASONExplain one clear reason. Start with because, since, or as.
E
EXAMPLESupport with a personal example, habit, or specific memory.
THE 3-SECOND RULE

Begin speaking within 3 seconds of hearing the question. Use one opener to bridge the gap if needed:

Well, I think…natural, common starter
To be honest…shows authenticity
Actually…signals a considered opinion
In my case…personalises the answer
If my memory serves me right…useful for past events
⚠ DO NOT open every answer with the same phrase — the examiner will notice.

3-Second Response Drill
Timed speaking — your teacher asks, you must start within 3 seconds
Your teacher will ask a question from any section of this worksheet. Your task is to begin speaking within 3 seconds without preparation.
  • Started ≤3 sec: Did you open your mouth and begin before 3 seconds had passed?
  • Used A.R.E: Did your answer contain an Answer, a Reason, and an Example?
  • Spoke clearly: Could the listener understand every word without straining?
  • Finished naturally: Did you come to a smooth stop, or trail off awkwardly?
